- Competitor Reference: title, thumbnail, URL, duration, description, and public metadata
- Full Competitor Transcript
- Optional Channel Learnings

MISSION
Analyze the competitor's execution without rewriting it and without fact-checking from memory.

DELIVER
1. SUBJECT
Identify the actual event, person, civilization, or historical question.
2. COMPETITOR PROMISE
Explain what the title and thumbnail promise and whether the opening fulfills it quickly.
3. NARRATIVE MAP
Map: hook, setup, escalation, turning point, climax, aftermath, ending.
4. STRONG IDEA-LEVEL ELEMENTS
Extract only concepts worth researching:
- contradiction;- human stakes;- emotional tension;- visually strong moments;
- curiosity beats.

Identify sponsor, filler, repetition, long background, generic moralizing, non-transferable jokes, and flat chronology.
6. CLAIM MAP
List every claim requiring independent verification:
- names and relationships;- dates and locations;- casualty counts and numbers;- motives and internal states;- quotations and dialogue;- “common belief” claims;- dramatic details;- later memoir claims.7.
